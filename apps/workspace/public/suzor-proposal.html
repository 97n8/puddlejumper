02  ·  The Partnership
The Partnership

Structural Risk in Municipal Operations

Massachusetts municipalities under 25,000 residents share a structural vulnerability: when a key employee leaves, institutional knowledge leaves with them. IT infrastructure stays. Governance does not. The result is delayed public records responses, missed compliance windows, dropped grant cycles, and staff set up to fail from day one. Compounding this, most municipalities are locked into legacy systems — including Tyler Technologies (Munis), Patriot Properties, and VADAR — that cannot be replaced without significant disruption. Suzor IT has been solving the infrastructure half of this problem for years. PublicLogic was built to solve the governance half. SCS combines both into a single offering no competitor in this geography currently provides.

06  ·  Legal & Compliance
Legal & Compliance

Intellectual Property & License Scope

  • PublicLogic retains 100% ownership of VAULT, LogicOS, CivicPulse, and all derivatives regardless of authorship during the partnership.
  • Suzor IT receives a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to deliver, install, configure, and support PL-licensed VAULT modules to SCS clients only.
  • All bugfixes, patches, or enhancements authored by either party are automatically assigned to PL upon creation; Suzor IT retains a license to use within SCS deployments during the term.
  • No sublicensing, white-labeling, or modification of core VAULT framework without PL's prior written consent.
  • Config/documentation escrow maintained by PL for client continuity. Credentials transferred to a designated successor within 14 days of Systems Architect departure.

Exclusivity & Remedy Schedule

PL designates Suzor IT as exclusive VAULT delivery partner for MA and NH municipalities and K–12 districts, conditioned on the $36,000 Annual Minimum commencing Year 2. Exclusivity does not apply to PL's existing clients as of the Effective Date, statewide procurement vehicles, higher education, or municipalities Suzor IT declines in writing.

If the Annual Minimum Is Missed

Failure to meet Annual Minimum: PL issues written notice. Suzor IT has 90 days to cure by demonstrating a signed pipeline sufficient to meet the minimum within 180 days.

Cure expires without cure: PL may (a) invite other delivery partners for new engagements, or (b) carve out specific municipalities as non-exclusive. Existing SCS clients unaffected.

Two consecutive missed years: PL may convert exclusivity to non-exclusive on 30 days' written notice.

Data Custody & Cyber Obligations

All client data owned by the municipality. SCS does not act as records custodian. Complex redactions and legal holds excluded from PRR compliance calculations. Both parties comply with MGL Ch.66, Ch.30B, and applicable federal privacy law.

Data Breach Protocol

Notification: Both parties notify each other and affected municipalities within 72 hours of discovering a confirmed or reasonably suspected breach.

Forensics: Mandatory investigation at cost of the breaching party (shared if cause is indeterminate).

Annual attestation: Suzor IT provides SOC 2 Type II or equivalent pen-test certification scoped to SCS infrastructure. PL receives copies of all reports and remediation plans.

Uncapped exposure: Breach remediation costs attributable to Suzor IT infrastructure are not subject to the liability cap.

Staff Certification

  • All Suzor IT staff delivering VAULT modules must hold VAULT Foundations Certification before any client-facing work.
  • Certification: 8 hours of training, 80%-pass written assessment, and one supervised pilot engagement — designed and administered by the Division Head.
  • Minimum 3 certified staff required before Phase 1 pilot launches. Annual recertification: 4-hour refresher when new modules activate.

Phase 1 Acceptance Criteria

PRR Measurement Methodology

Time starts when a compliant request is received in the LogicOS PRR module. Time stops at delivery of response or written extension notice to requestor. Excluded: legal hold periods, requestor-caused delay, complex redactions requiring legal review, and requests received after 4:30 PM on Business Days (counted next Business Day). Measured over the first 60 days of pilot operation.

28-day clean run: Calendar days from first go-live with zero P1 incidents. P1 re-classification requires joint agreement of Systems Architect and Division Head within 24 hours of incident close.

Acceptance sign-off: Joint written checklist signed by Nathan R. Boudreau and Adam Suzor, with attached evidence package: test cases, incident log, compliance rate report, pen test report, and client satisfaction survey results.
