# Funding Volatility Register

**The volatile (competitive-discretionary) layer: what could kill each program, the early-warning signals, and which
bedrock source absorbs the loss.**

Companion to `federal_funding_architecture.md`. Verification date for time-sensitive findings: **2026-06-03**.

> This register exists because the architecture's promise is that it survives the loss of the *entire* volatile layer.
> Each row names the kill mechanism, the signals that precede it, and the **bedrock substitute** that keeps the cycle
> running. Not legal/accounting advice.

---

## How to read this register

- **Kill mechanism** is graded against the four-link chain (authorization → appropriation → allocation → obligation):
  a program "dies" when a link breaks, and *which* link tells you how fast and how reversible it is.
- **Early-warning signals** are the observable things that precede the break — watch these, not press releases.
- **Bedrock absorber** is the specific durable source the architecture shifts to. If a row's absorber is "own-source,"
  the loss is fully survivable by design.

**The three teachers (why this register exists):**

| Program | What looked alive | Which link broke | The lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Digital Equity Act** | $2.75B, fully authorized & appropriated under IIJA | **Allocation/obligation** — terminated 5/9/2025 as "unconstitutional" | Even appropriated money can be killed by executive action mid-stream. **DEAD — do not resurrect.** |
| **FEMA BRIC** | Appropriated from the Disaster Relief Fund | **Allocation** — agency tried to terminate 4/4/2025; courts forced restoration | Litigation can revive it, but slowly and unpredictably; never build a critical path on it. |
| **SLCGP** | IIJA-authorized, ran 4 years | **Authorization then appropriation** — expired 9/30/2025; FY2026 extended authority but **no new money** | A program can be "reauthorized" in spirit (PILLAR Act) and still have **zero dollars** for new awards. |

## Register

### 1. FEMA BRIC (AL 97.047) — IN-FLUX (reopening, fragile)

- **Status (verified 2026-06-03):** Terminated 4/4/2025; permanently enjoined 12/11/2025; enforcement order 3/6/2026;
  **FEMA issued a joint FY2024–2025 BRIC NOFO on 3/25/2026 with ~$1B available**
  ([Coastal Review](https://coastalreview.org/2026/03/federal-judge-orders-fema-to-restore-bric-program/)).
- **Kill mechanism:** Executive termination at the **allocation** link; the program is appropriated but the agency is
  a reluctant administrator under court order. A future administration could attempt re-termination or slow-walk
  obligation of awarded funds.
- **Early-warning signals:** New FEMA leadership statements reframing BRIC as "wasteful/politicized" (the 2025
  language); missed court-ordered status-report deadlines; NOFO published but obligations not flowing; appeals of the
  injunction.
- **Bedrock absorber:** **HMGP (AL 97.039)** — statutory, auto-funded as a percentage of declared-disaster costs, not
  competitive; plus **CWSRF/DWSRF** for the water-resilience portion, and **own-source capital** for the remainder.
  *The architecture treats BRIC as upside; the resilience critical path rides HMGP + SRF + own-source.*

### 2. SLCGP (AL 97.137) — IN-FLUX (authorized, unfunded for new awards)

- **Status (verified 2026-06-03):** Authorization extended to 9/30/2026 by FY2026 appropriations, but **no new money
  for new grants**; **PILLAR Act (H.R.5078) passed House 11/17/2025**, Senate S.3251 in committee, **not enacted as of
  6/3/2026** ([StateTech](https://statetechmagazine.com/article/2025/11/congress-revives-state-and-local-cyber-grants-funding-remains-unclear)).
  Prior-year/IIJA carryover NOFOs still flow at the state level.
- **Kill mechanism:** **Appropriation** link — the cleanest example in the catalog of "authorized but no money."
- **Early-warning signals:** PILLAR Act stalls in the Senate; FY2027 appropriations omit a dollar figure; state
  administrative agencies stop issuing new sub-NOFOs as carryover exhausts; rising local match (10→40% over the
  original 4 years) deters applicants.
- **Bedrock absorber:** **Community Compact IT** (MA, no match — funds the cyber-relevant IT modernization directly)
  and **own-source IT capital**; the **compliance/governance** portion of cyber work is a PublicLogic professional
  service that can ride *any* of those sources, not just SLCGP. Where cyber is part of a broader modernization, fold it
  into a **CDBG** or **MassWorks**-funded project scope.

### 3. BEAD (AL 11.041) — IN-FLUX (restructured)

- **Status:** Restructured in 2025 ("Benefit of the Bargain"/tech-neutral); funds obligated to state broadband
  offices but local subgrant timing shifted. (CANDIDATE on specific round timing — verify with the state office.)
- **Kill mechanism:** **Allocation/obligation** — money is appropriated (IIJA advance) but subgrant rules and timing
  are executive-controlled and were rewritten mid-program.
- **Early-warning signals:** State broadband office re-running its subgrant round; changed scoring criteria; provider
  challenges; shifting "unserved/underserved" definitions.
- **Bedrock absorber:** **USDA ReConnect** (rural, standing) and **own-source/municipal-bond** broadband for the
  highest-priority gaps; **Digital Equity Act is DEAD** and is *not* an absorber — do not budget against it.

### 4. EDA Public Works / EAA, USDOT RAISE, SS4A (AL 11.300/11.307, 20.933, 20.939) — CANDIDATE/volatile

- **Status:** Standing competitive programs; specific FY2026 NOFOs/levels not re-verified this session (CANDIDATE).
  SS4A is IIJA-funded through FY2026.
- **Kill mechanism:** **Appropriation + executive discretion** — annual competitive lines whose criteria shift with
  administration priorities; classic volatile layer.
- **Early-warning signals:** NOFO criteria reweighted (e.g., away from climate/equity factors); award pause pending
  reorganization; reduced appropriation in the THUD/EDA bills; PWEDA reauthorization lapse.
- **Bedrock absorber:** **CDBG** (formula) for economic-development and public-facility scope; **CWSRF/DWSRF** for the
  water/infrastructure portion; **MassWorks** (state) and **own-source capital** for the balance.

### 5. IMLS Grants to States (AL 45.310), NHPRC (AL 89.003) — IN-FLUX / CANDIDATE

- **Status:** IMLS targeted for reduction by 2025 executive action and litigation (IN-FLUX); NHPRC is a small standing
  grants program (CANDIDATE on FY2026 cycle).
- **Kill mechanism:** **Allocation** (IMLS — agency-level reduction attempts) and **appropriation** (small programs are
  easy line-item cuts).
- **Early-warning signals:** Executive orders naming the agency; RIFs/leadership departures; grants-to-states formula
  payments delayed; FY2026 enacted level omitting or zeroing the account.
- **Bedrock absorber:** **Community Compact IT** (records/digitization as IT modernization), **own-source** records and
  archives budgets, and — for records tied to a larger system — folding the records-stewardship scope into a
  CDBG/MassWorks-funded modernization. *Records stewardship is a PublicLogic professional service that survives the
  loss of any single grant.*

### 6. 48E / 45Y clean-electricity credits (IRC §48E/§45Y) — IN-FLUX (closing window)

- **Status (verified 2026-06-03):** OBBBA **terminates** wind/solar for facilities placed in service after 12/31/2027;
  **full credit only if construction begins before 7/4/2026**; FEOC material-assistance (MACR) thresholds bite from
  2026 (IRS Notice 2026-15, 2/12/2026) ([Novogradac](https://www.novoco.com/periodicals/articles/obbba-and-the-clean-energy-race-against-the-clock)).
- **Kill mechanism:** **Statutory sunset** — unusual for a tax credit; the date is fixed in law, so the "kill" is a
  *deadline*, not discretion.
- **Early-warning signals:** The calendar itself — **7/4/2026 begin-construction** and **12/31/2027 placed-in-service**;
  FEOC supply-chain disqualification (a Chinese/Russian/etc. component pushing the project below the MACR threshold);
  delayed Treasury guidance creating safe-harbor uncertainty.
- **Bedrock absorber:** **§45Q** (preserved and *strengthened* to $85/ton, 12-yr period) for carbon projects, and
  **NMTC** (now permanent) for the capital stack. *Anchor clean-energy/environmental deals on §45Q + NMTC, treat
  48E/45Y as a time-boxed bonus, and front-load any 48E/45Y project to begin construction before 7/4/2026.*

### 7. ARPA SLFRF (AL 21.027) — IN-FLUX (terminal wind-down)

- **Status (verified 2026-06-03):** Obligation deadline **passed** (12/31/2024); **expend by 12/31/2026**; closeout
  report 4/30/2027; unspent funds returned to Treasury
  ([UNC Coates' Canons](https://canons.sog.unc.edu/2026/01/2026-the-final-countdown-has-begun-for-arp-slfrf/)).
- **Kill mechanism:** **Obligation deadline already passed** — this isn't a risk, it's a countdown. No new work can be
  started; the only question is whether already-obligated funds are spent in time.
- **Early-warning signals:** Client projects slipping past 12/31/2026; unspent obligated balances; missing
  Project & Expenditure reports.
- **Bedrock absorber:** **Own-source** for anything that cannot finish before 12/31/2026. *Use SLFRF only to fund
  Steward work that completes inside the window; never to start a new build.*

### 8. Standing federal block/formula grants treated as CANDIDATE (CDBG, USDA RD, EECBG, SAMHSA MHBG/SUBG, WIOA)

- **Status:** Real, long-standing, formula-distributed — but their **exact FY2026 appropriation levels were not
  re-verified this session** (CANDIDATE). They are *more* durable than the competitive layer (formula distribution
  resists executive discretion), but appropriation levels still move year to year.
- **Kill mechanism:** **Appropriation level** (rarely zeroed; more often trimmed) and, for reauthorization-dependent
  programs (WIOA), an authorization lapse that continues under appropriations riders.
- **Early-warning signals:** Continuing resolutions at reduced levels; reauthorization stalls; formula reallocations;
  set-aside percentage changes (e.g., SAMHSA crisis/prevention set-asides).
- **Bedrock absorber:** Each other and **own-source** — these *are* the bedrock that absorbs the volatile layer. Their
  own residual risk (appropriation trims) is absorbed by **entitlements (Medicaid Title XIX)**, **tax credits**, and
  **own-source revenue**, which do not depend on annual appropriation at all.

## Mitigation summary — the absorber map

| If this volatile source is lost… | …the architecture shifts the critical path to (bedrock) |
|---|---|
| BRIC (resilience capital) | HMGP (statutory) + CWSRF/DWSRF + own-source capital |
| SLCGP (cyber) | Community Compact IT (no match) + own-source IT + CDBG-folded scope |
| BEAD (broadband) | USDA ReConnect + own-source/municipal bond (NOT the DEAD Digital Equity Act) |
| EDA / RAISE / SS4A (competitive infra) | CDBG (formula) + CWSRF/DWSRF + MassWorks + own-source |
| IMLS / NHPRC (records) | Community Compact IT + own-source + records scope folded into modernization |
| 48E/45Y (clean electricity, sunsetting) | §45Q ($85/ton, statutory) + NMTC (permanent) |
| ARPA SLFRF (closing) | Own-source for anything past 12/31/2026 |
| CDBG/RD/EECBG/SAMHSA/WIOA trims | Medicaid Title XIX (entitlement) + tax credits + own-source |

**Bottom line:** every volatile row has a bedrock absorber, and the terminal absorber for the whole register is the
same triad — **statutory entitlements, statutory tax credits, and municipal own-source revenue** — none of which
depends on annual appropriation or executive discretion. That is why the architecture stands when the volatile layer
is removed.
