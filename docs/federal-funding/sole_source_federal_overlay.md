# Sole-Source Justification — Federal Overlay (2 CFR Part 200)

**A companion to PublicLogic's existing MGL c.30B §7 sole-source justification, for use when *federal* funds pay
for PublicLogic's services.**

Builds directly on the existing templates (`03_SoleSource_Shrewsbury.docx`, `Sutton_SoleSource.docx`). Those are
grounded in **Massachusetts G.L. c.30B §7** ("unique and specialized expertise"). When the money paying PublicLogic
is *state or local own-source* (as in Sutton — *"No MBI grant funds are paid to PublicLogic… paid from Town
appropriation"*), c.30B §7 **alone governs** and this overlay is not triggered. When the money is a **federal award**
(CWSRF, CDBG, BRIC, EDA, ARPA SLFRF, USDA RD, etc.) or federal funds passed through the Commonwealth, the federal
procurement standards (**2 CFR §§200.317–.327**) apply **on top of** c.30B §7, and the noncompetitive award must
satisfy them too. This document supplies that overlay.

Verification date: **2026-06-03**. For Town Counsel review; retain in the contract file. **Not legal advice** —
allowability and procurement compliance are subject to the funding source, the approved budget, the governing cost
principles, and Town Counsel's determination.

---

## 0. The decision rule — which procurement regime applies

| What pays for PublicLogic's services | Governing procurement law | This overlay? |
|---|---|---|
| Town appropriation / own-source / unrestricted funds | **MGL c.30B §7** only | **No** — c.30B §7 justification stands alone |
| A **state** grant with its own procurement rules (e.g., Community Compact) | c.30B §7 + the grant's terms | Usually no federal overlay (confirm the grant isn't federally sourced) |
| A **federal** award, or federal funds passed through the Commonwealth | c.30B §7 **and** 2 CFR §§200.317–.327 | **Yes** — attach this overlay |

> **Why it matters:** A c.30B §7 determination that is perfectly valid under state law is **not sufficient** to make
> a noncompetitive award allowable against a federal grant. If a Single Audit or a federal/pass-through monitor
> reviews the file and finds only the state justification, the cost can be **questioned or disallowed**. The fix is
> cheap: document the federal circumstance, the cost/price analysis, and the conflict-of-interest mitigation in the
> same file.

## 1. Federal authority for a noncompetitive (sole-source) award — 2 CFR §200.320(c)

Under the 2024-revised Uniform Guidance, a noncompetitive procurement is allowable **only if at least one** of these
circumstances applies (§200.320(c)):

1. aggregate amount ≤ the **micro-purchase threshold** ($10,000; self-certifiable up to $50,000);
2. the transaction **can only be fulfilled by a single source**;
3. **public exigency/emergency** that won't permit the delay of a competitive solicitation;
4. the recipient **requests in writing** and the **Federal agency or pass-through entity gives written approval**; or
5. after **soliciting several sources**, competition is determined **inadequate**.

([2 CFR §200.320, eCFR](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-2/subtitle-A/chapter-II/part-200/subpart-D/subject-group-ECFR45ddd4419ad436d/section-200.320), verified 2026-06-03.)

**PublicLogic's pathway is §200.320(c)(2) — single source.** PublicLogic's existing three c.30B §7 grounds *are* the
single-source showing; they map one-to-one:

| Existing c.30B §7 ground | Maps to federal §200.320(c)(2) single-source showing |
|---|---|
| **Ground 1** — governance architecture (VAULT modules mapped to MGL authority) purpose-built for *this* town's operational environment | The required deliverable does not exist as a market commodity; it exists only in PublicLogic's town-specific configuration → single source |
| **Ground 2** — integrated municipal-policy + custom-code + CFIR/behavioral-systems capability not available as a single market offering | No other single vendor can fulfill the integrated requirement → single source |
| **Ground 3** — non-replicable institutional knowledge of the town's operations | A competitor would have to recreate the knowledge, making competition impracticable → single source / inadequate competition (also supports (c)(5)) |

> For a **federal** file, restate the determination explicitly in §200.320(c)(2) terms: *"The Town determines this
> transaction can only be fulfilled by a single source, PublicLogic LLC, for the reasons documented in Grounds 1–3 of
> the attached MGL c.30B §7 justification, which are incorporated here by reference."*

## 2. The three things the federal overlay adds (that the state template does not carry)

### 2.1 Cost or price analysis — §200.324 (and §200.404 reasonableness always)

- **Over the Simplified Acquisition Threshold ($250,000):** the Town **must** perform a **cost or price analysis for
  every procurement, *including noncompetitive ones***, and **must make an independent cost estimate *before*
  receiving PublicLogic's proposal** (§200.324(a)). Costs are allowable only to the extent they would be allowable
  under Subpart E (cost principles).
- **At or below the SAT** (e.g., Shrewsbury's $123,000 implementation component): §200.324's formal analysis is **not
  mandated by the threshold**, but the cost must still be **reasonable** under **§200.404** and **§200.459**
  (professional-service costs). The existing **"Cost Reasonableness" table is the right artifact** — for a federal
  file, add (a) an **independent Town estimate made before the proposal**, and (b) a one-line allowability cite to
  §200.459.
- **Indirect costs / rate:** if any indirect is charged, the Town may apply the **15% de minimis MTDC** rate
  (§200.414(f), effective for awards on/after 10/1/2024) absent a negotiated rate. PublicLogic's fee rides as a
  **direct** professional-service cost, so this typically does not apply to PublicLogic's line.

([2 CFR §200.324, eCFR](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-2/subtitle-A/chapter-II/part-200/subpart-D/subject-group-ECFR45ddd4419ad436d/section-200.324), verified 2026-06-03.)

### 2.2 Conflict of interest — §200.318(c) and §200.319(b) (the sharp one: the dual role)

The existing templates disclose the **dual role** under **MGL c.268A / c.30B** (Boudreau is PublicLogic's founder
*and* helped develop the application and the procurement justification; he is **not** the Town's procurement officer;
the Town Manager independently reviews; Town Counsel signs off). The federal overlay must add two things:

- **§200.318(c) — written standards of conduct.** The Town (pass-through/recipient) must **maintain a written
  conflict-of-interest policy** covering employees engaged in selection/award/administration of contracts. Confirm the
  Town's policy is on file and referenced.
- **§200.319(b) — the organizational-conflict / unfair-advantage rule.** *"A contractor that develops or drafts
  specifications, requirements, statements of work, or invitations for bids or requests for proposals must be excluded
  from competing for such procurements."* Because PublicLogic participated in developing the grant application and the
  procurement justification, a federal reviewer will look here. **Mitigation to document in the file:**
  1. The award is a **§200.320(c)(2) single-source** procurement — there is **no competition** in which an unfair
     advantage could be gained; §200.319(b) addresses *competitive* procurements.
  2. PublicLogic's application/justification work was a **fixed-fee, non-contingent professional service**
     (§200.459(b) — contingent fees are a factor *against* allowability; PublicLogic charges the fee **regardless of
     award**), so there is no incentive structure that biases the specification toward a win.
  3. The **single-source determination was made independently by the Town Manager** (not the procurement officer
     conflict), with **Town Counsel review** — the same mitigation the state template already records, now mapped to
     the federal standard.
  4. Where practical, the Town **documents the requirement in its own words** (an independent Town statement of need),
     so the specification of record is the Town's, not PublicLogic's.

([2 CFR §200.318–.319, eCFR](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-2/subtitle-A/chapter-II/part-200/subpart-D/subject-group-ECFR45ddd4419ad436d), verified 2026-06-03.)

### 2.3 Contractor (not subrecipient) classification — §200.331

The existing templates already establish the facts that make PublicLogic a **contractor**, not a subrecipient: the
Town **owns all code/configurations/documentation at close**, PublicLogic **retains no role, access, or recurring
fees**, and the services are provided within PublicLogic's normal business to many municipalities in a competitive
market. For the federal file, state the §200.331 conclusion explicitly: *"PublicLogic is a **contractor** under
§200.331; it does not make programmatic/eligibility determinations and is not subject to the federal program's
compliance requirements in its own right. It is therefore procured under §§200.317–.327 and is not itself
Single-Audited."* (The **Town** has the Single Audit obligation only if it expends ≥ **$1,000,000** in federal awards
in its fiscal year, §200.501.)

### 2.4 A note on the grant-writing hours themselves — §200.460

Distinct from the *implementation* contract: the cost of **preparing the grant application** (proposal preparation) is
treated as an **indirect (F&A) cost under §200.460** and is **not** chargeable to the federal award being sought
(won or not). Keep the writing fee on the Town's **own-source / unrestricted / F&A** line and **non-contingent**; the
allowable federal charge is the **implementation** contract (this overlay) and **post-award grant administration** —
not the application-writing. (See `grant_writing_hours.md` / the `GrantWriting_Hours` sheet for the worked hours.)

## 4. What this means for the funding architecture (cross-reference)

This overlay is the **procurement pathway** that lets PublicLogic's non-contingent professional-service fee actually
ride inside federal money — the mechanism assumed throughout `federal_funding_architecture.md` (§1.1, "subrecipient
vs. contractor" and "procurement standards"). The chain is:

**Stewardship Map / narrative (own-source, §200.460, non-contingent)** → **federal award won** → **implementation
services procured single-source under c.30B §7 + §200.320(c)(2), cost-reasonable under §200.459, COI-mitigated under
§200.319(b)** → **PublicLogic delivers; Town owns everything at close (§200.331 contractor)** → **own-source
stewardship handoff**. The Shrewsbury and Sutton justifications are the live precedent for the middle link; this
overlay makes that link hold when the dollars are federal.
