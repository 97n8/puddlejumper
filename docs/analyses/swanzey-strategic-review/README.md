# Swanzey Strategic Review — *Restraint and Its Limits*

A board-facing strategic review that reads the 15-year record and makes the case for **what to do
differently and why** — gracious in tone, rich in data, and concrete in tools. Built to be **cut back**
to whatever the board judges realistic.

## The argument in one line

> Swanzey **leads with restraint**. Restraint built deep voter trust for a decade — and, used the same
> way for another decade, it will starve the Town's capital and erode the very trust it built. Don't
> abandon restraint; **upgrade the instrument**: save first, leverage the money already on the table,
> and tell residents the plain truth. *Earn the right to change, then earn the right to spend.*

## How it's organized (10 sections)

1. Purpose & posture — *a mirror, not a critique*
2. Executive summary — the inflection, in five numbers
3. **Credit first** — what the town did right; why none of the strain is the board's fault
4. The pattern — *the town leads with restraint* (3 ways, with charts)
5. The hidden cost — what restraint quietly decreases
6. The inflection — the diagnosis, *stated plainly and kindly*
7. **Earning the right to change** — incl. the "what we'd do differently & why" table
8. **Earning the right to spend** — the financing upgrade (62% other people's money; the glide path)
9. Practical toolkit — reserve ladder, decision rules, continuity compact, tax-bill explainer
10. Landing it — a 12-month plan and the one-paragraph ask
