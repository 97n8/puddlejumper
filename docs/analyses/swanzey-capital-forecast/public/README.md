# Public Communications Pack — Board Cover Memo

**To:** Select Board, Budget Committee, CIP Committee
**Re:** Public-education materials for the capital plan
**Purpose:** Adopt and deploy — these are the tools that put the plan in front of voters *before* the
ballot.

The capital plan only works if voters understand it before they vote (you can't explain at the SB2
ballot box). This folder is the ready-to-deploy public-facing layer of the [Capital Forecast
Book](../CAPITAL-FORECAST.md). Three pieces, three jobs:

| File | Job | Deploy via |
|---|---|---|
| [`voter-flyer.md`](./voter-flyer.md) | 30-second hook — the headline numbers | Print one-pager for doors, the transfer station, the polls; Town website |
| [`FAQ.md`](./FAQ.md) | Answer the predictable objections, calmly and on the record | Town website; information-night handout; counter at Town Hall |
| [`op-ed.md`](./op-ed.md) | Make the public case in the town's paper | Submit to the Keene Sentinel (~660 words) |

### How they fit the rollout

1. **Board adopts** the CIP and the [model warrant articles](../presentation/warrant-articles.md).
2. **Publish** the flyer + FAQ on the Town website and post the flyer around town.
3. **Op-ed** runs ~2–3 weeks before the deliberative session.
4. **Information night**: present the [deck](../presentation/deliberative-session-deck.md); hand out the
   flyer and FAQ.
5. **Ballot**: simple-majority reserve votes — nothing to lose at 3/5.

### Before anything goes out

- [ ] Confirm the **recount tally**, **bridge-aid eligibility/inspection status**, the **water system's
      legal status** (town vs. precinct), and **every dollar figure** with staff and DRA.
- [ ] Run public materials past **town counsel** for any required disclaimers.
- [ ] Fill in the **byline** and any **committee attribution** on the flyer and op-ed.
- [ ] These are **model/planning materials** — the Town owns the final numbers and language.

> Consistent message across all three: **62% other people's money · no new bonds · no 3/5 votes · a
> tank of oil a year, briefly, then less than today.** Say it the same way everywhere.
