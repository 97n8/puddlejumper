# Swanzey Capital Forecast Book

**How to fund every capital need the Town of Swanzey has — without another failed bond vote.**

A 10-year (FY2027–FY2036) capital forecast and financing strategy that takes the town's whole capital
backlog — the failed $1.2M public-works facility, the Christian Hill Road bridge, Lower Winchester
Street, the West Swanzey water system, and the Town Hall ADA project — and **funds all of it.**

## The answer in three numbers

- **$5.275M** of capital needs — **solved.**
- **62%** paid with **non-property-tax money** (bridge aid, water-rate/SRF financing, grants).
- **Peak local impact ≈ +$75/yr** on a $300k home for two years — then the capital burden falls
  **below today's** with everything built. **Zero new bonds. Zero 3/5 votes.**

## The one idea

> You don't beat a failed 3/5 bond with a better speech — you **restructure the money** so the scary
> vote disappears. Make other people pay 62% (RSA 234 bridge aid, DWSRF water-rate financing, grants),
> convert the local remainder from a 3/5 **bond** into majority-vote **reserves** (RSA 35), and phase
> the deposits so the rate glides instead of spikes.

> Provenance: funding mechanics and statutory shares are sourced `[R]`; project costs and the rate
> glide are planning estimates `[M]` calibrated to Swanzey's published totals. The strategy is the
> deliverable; confirm the digits with engineering, NHDOT/NHDES, and DRA before adoption.
