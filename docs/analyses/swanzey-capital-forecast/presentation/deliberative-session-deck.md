# Why last year's vote failed

It wasn't the project. It was the **structure**.

| The old way | The problem |
|---|---|
| One big **bond** | Needs a **3/5 supermajority** — 55% "yes" still loses |
| One **$1.2M** number | Reads as a tax bomb |
| **100% local** | We paid full price when the state would pay most |
| **Cold vote** | No plan voters had seen before |

### A failed bond is a *money-structure* problem — so we fixed the structure.

<!--

# The one idea

## Don't re-argue the vote. **Restructure the money.**

1. **Make other people pay first** — state & federal money
2. **Save instead of borrow** — reserves, not bonds
3. **Smooth it out** — small steady steps, never a spike

<!--
Slow down here. This is the thesis. Three moves. Everything else is detail.
-->

# Move 1 — Other people pay 62%

| Project | Total | Who else pays | Our share |
|---|---|---|---|
| Christian Hill Bridge | $1.0M | **State 80%** ($800k) | $200k |
| West Swanzey Water | $2.08M | **Water-rate/SRF + grants** | ~$0 *(taxes)* |
| Lower Winchester St | $0.75M | Highway aid ($270k) | $480k |
| Town Hall ADA | $0.25M | **Grant** ($125k) | $125k |
| Public Works Facility | $1.2M | — | $1.2M |

### Of **$5.3M**, only **$2.0M** is local. **62% is someone else's money.**

# Move 2 — Save, don't borrow

| | **Bond** (old way) | **Capital Reserve** (new way) |
|---|---|---|
| Vote needed | **3/5 supermajority** | **Simple majority** |
| Interest | Yes | **None** |
| Risk | Lose at 3/5, project dies | Save up, pay cash |

### We build the Public Works Facility **pay-as-you-go** — finished by **2031, with no bond.**

<!--
"A capital reserve fund is just a town savings account, approved by a simple majority. We set aside

# Project by project — how each gets done

- 🌉 **Bridge** → State pays 80%; we bank the 20% share in a Bridge Reserve
- 💧 **Water** → State loan repaid by water rates + grants — off your tax bill
- 🛣️ **Lower Winchester** → Highway aid + a Roads Reserve
- ♿ **ADA** → Community grant + a Facilities Reserve
- 🏢 **Public Works Facility** → Pay-as-you-go from its own Reserve, built by 2031

### Plus reserves for trucks, fire apparatus, and buildings — so we're **never caught off guard again.**

- The plan **funds** the bridge — 80% state, 20% saved by the town

- That timeline **matches** the petition to study it for a year
- **The plan and the petition want the same runway — not a fight**

**Capital Reserve Funds** (simple majority each):

- Public Works Facility · Bridges · Roads
- Highway Equipment · Fire Apparatus · Facilities · Technology

**Plus authorizations** to chase the outside money:

- State Bridge Aid (RSA 234) · Water SRF (NHDES) · Community grants

### Year 1 deposits total **$520,000** — and unlock **millions** in outside funding.

# Why this is safe

- Every reserve is a **majority** vote — easy to start, easy to pause
- Bridge money is **contingent on state aid** — no aid, no town cost
- Water repays through **rates**, not taxes
- A published **Capital Plan** — no more surprises
- Miss a year? The plan **slows; it never dies**
