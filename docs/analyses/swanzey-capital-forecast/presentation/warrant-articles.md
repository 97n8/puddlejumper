# Model Warrant Articles — Swanzey Capital Plan

**Status: DRAFT model language for discussion.** Finalize with **town counsel** and submit to the
**DRA Municipal Services "pre-review"** (optional but recommended — they flag language that would be
disallowed). Numbers track Year 1 (FY2027) of the [ten-year plan](../ten-year-plan.csv). In an SB2
town these go on the warrant, are debated/amended at the **deliberative session**, and decided by
**official ballot** in March. Each carries **recommendation tags** (Select Board, and Budget Committee
if the town has one).

> **Why the wording is fussy:** DRA verifies enacted article language against the meeting minutes and
> will **disallow** an article that is improperly constructed (wrong fund verb, missing agent
> designation, ambiguous purpose). "Establish … and raise and appropriate … and appoint … as agents
> to expend" is the canonical RSA 35 construction. Don't paraphrase it.

---

## Group A — Establish the capital reserve funds (the engine of the plan)

Each is a **special warrant article** (RSA 32:3, VI) — non-lapsing, dedicated purpose. Each is a
**simple-majority** vote (RSA 35), *not* the 3/5 a bond would need.

### Article A-1 — Public Works Facility Capital Reserve Fund

> To see if the Town will vote to establish a **Public Works Facility Capital Reserve Fund** under the
> provisions of **RSA 35:1** for the purpose of **planning, designing, constructing, reconstructing, or
> acquiring a public works facility and site improvements**, and to raise and appropriate the sum of
> **One Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars ($150,000)** to be placed in this fund; further, to name the
> **Board of Selectmen as agents to expend** from said fund pursuant to **RSA 35:15**.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

*Drafting notes:* (1) Purpose stated by **type** ("a public works facility") gives flexibility while
remaining specific enough for DRA. (2) Naming agents to expend (RSA 35:15) lets the board build the
facility from the fund **without a second 3/5 bond vote** — this is the structural move that retires
the failed-bond problem. (3) This article alone commits the town to nothing it cannot later stop.

### Article A-2 — Bridge Capital Reserve Fund

> To see if the Town will vote to establish a **Bridge Capital Reserve Fund** under **RSA 35:1** for the
> purpose of **the municipal share of the construction, reconstruction, rehabilitation, or replacement
> of Town bridges, including amounts required to match State Bridge Aid under RSA 234**, and to raise
> and appropriate the sum of **Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000)** to be placed in this fund; further, to
> name the **Board of Selectmen as agents to expend**.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

*Drafting note:* Naming the **RSA 234 match** in the purpose makes the leverage explicit — voters are
banking a 20% share against an 80% state contribution for the Christian Hill Road bridge.

### Article A-3 — Highway Reconstruction Capital Reserve Fund

> To see if the Town will vote to establish a **Highway Reconstruction Capital Reserve Fund** under
> **RSA 35:1** for the purpose of **reconstruction and improvement of Town roads and related drainage,
> including the Lower Winchester Street corridor**, and to raise and appropriate the sum of **One
> Hundred Thousand Dollars ($100,000)** to be placed in this fund; further, to name the **Board of
> Selectmen as agents to expend**.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

### Article A-4 — Highway Equipment Capital Reserve Fund

> To see if the Town will vote to establish a **Highway Equipment Capital Reserve Fund** under **RSA
> 35:1** for the purpose of **the purchase or replacement of highway department vehicles and
> equipment**, and to raise and appropriate the sum of **Ninety Thousand Dollars ($90,000)** to be
> placed in this fund; further, to name the **Board of Selectmen as agents to expend**.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

### Article A-5 — Fire Apparatus Capital Reserve Fund

> To see if the Town will vote to establish a **Fire Apparatus Capital Reserve Fund** under **RSA 35:1**
> for the purpose of **the purchase or replacement of fire apparatus and related equipment**, and to
> raise and appropriate the sum of **Seventy Thousand Dollars ($70,000)** to be placed in this fund;
> further, to name the **Board of Selectmen as agents to expend**.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

### Article A-6 — Town Facilities Capital Reserve Fund

> To see if the Town will vote to establish a **Town Facilities Capital Reserve Fund** under **RSA 35:1**
> for the purpose of **the renovation, improvement, accessibility (ADA) compliance, and major repair of
> Town buildings, including Town Hall**, and to raise and appropriate the sum of **Forty Thousand
> Dollars ($40,000)** to be placed in this fund; further, to name the **Board of Selectmen as agents to
> expend**.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

### Article A-7 — Technology & Records Capital Reserve Fund

> To see if the Town will vote to establish a **Technology and Records Capital Reserve Fund** under
> **RSA 35:1** for the purpose of **acquisition and upgrade of information technology, geographic
> information systems, cyber-security, and records-management systems required to meet retention
> obligations under RSA 33-A**, and to raise and appropriate the sum of **Twenty Thousand Dollars
> ($20,000)** to be placed in this fund; further, to name the **Board of Selectmen as agents to
> expend**.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

**Group A total appropriation: $520,000** — all simple-majority, all non-lapsing, all dedicated.

## Group B — Leverage articles (turn on the outside money)

### Article B-1 — Christian Hill Road Bridge / State Bridge Aid authorization

> To see if the Town will vote to authorize the Select Board to **apply for, accept, and expend State
> Bridge Aid under RSA 234** for the reconstruction, rehabilitation, or replacement of the **Christian
> Hill Road bridge**, and to raise and appropriate the sum of **One Million Dollars ($1,000,000)** for
> this purpose, with **$800,000 (80%) to be funded by State Bridge Aid** and the Town's share of
> **$200,000 (20%)** to be funded by **withdrawal from the Bridge Capital Reserve Fund** (Article A-2),
> with no amount to be raised by **new** taxation in the current year; and to authorize the Select Board
> to take all actions necessary to carry out the project. **This appropriation is contingent upon
> receipt of State Bridge Aid.**
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

*Drafting notes:* (1) State the **80/20 split** and the **contingency on aid** so the Town is not on the
hook for the full amount if aid is delayed in the queue. (2) Fund the Town share **from the reserve**,
not new taxes — that's the whole strategy. (3) Confirm with NHDOT that the bridge is on the program
list and that **biennial inspection** is current before this goes forward. (4) Place this **after** the
Bridge CRF has had a year or two to accrue, or pair with a contingency that it executes only when the
reserve balance is sufficient.

### Article B-2 — West Swanzey Water System / DWSRF authorization

> To see if the Town will vote to authorize the Select Board to **apply for, accept, and expend loans
> and grants from the New Hampshire Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF) administered by NHDES**,
> including any **principal forgiveness** and **asset-management or strategic-planning grants**, for the
> evaluation, planning, and improvement of the **West Swanzey water system**; and to authorize repayment
> of any such loan through **water system user charges and rates** rather than general property taxation;
> and to authorize the Select Board to enter into the necessary loan agreements and to take all actions
> necessary to carry out the project.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

*Drafting notes:* (1) If the system is a separate **water precinct/district**, this article (and the
rate-setting authority) belongs to that district, not the Town — **verify the system's legal status
first.** (2) Routing repayment to **rates** is what keeps this off the property-tax rate. (3) A
companion **rate study** should precede or accompany the loan. (4) If a bond/note is the legal vehicle
for the SRF loan, this becomes an **RSA 33** article requiring **3/5** — confirm the structure with bond
counsel and NHDES; many SRF loans are authorized as notes under RSA 33 even when repaid by rates.

### Article B-3 — Town Hall ADA Accessibility / CDBG acceptance

> To see if the Town will vote to authorize the Select Board to **apply for, accept, and expend a
> Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) and/or other accessibility grant funds** for **ADA stairwell
> and accessibility improvements at Town Hall**, and to raise and appropriate the sum of **Two Hundred
> Fifty Thousand Dollars ($250,000)** for this purpose, with up to **$125,000 to be funded by grant**
> and the balance of **$125,000** to be funded by **withdrawal from the Town Facilities Capital Reserve
> Fund** (Article A-6), with no amount to be raised by **new** taxation in the current year.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board. Recommended by the Budget Committee.)*

## Group C — Optional / governance articles

### Article C-1 — Delegate default-budget preparation (housekeeping, if desired)

> *(Only if the town wishes the budget committee to prepare the default budget — requires a 3/5 vote
> under RSA 40:13, IX(b). Omit if the Select Board retains this function.)*

### Article C-2 — Capital Improvement Program adoption / continuation

> To see if the Town will vote to authorize the **Capital Improvement Program Committee**, under **RSA
> 674:5–8**, to **prepare and annually update a six-year-or-longer Capital Improvement Program** to
> guide the Town's capital reserve deposits and project sequencing, and to report the same to the Select
> Board and Budget Committee in advance of each annual budget.
> *(Recommended by the Select Board.)*

*Drafting note:* Swanzey already has a CIP Committee; this formalizes the published-CIP discipline that
makes every future capital ask an *expected* line rather than a surprise.

## Handling the petitioned articles (Christian Hill Road bridge)

The bridge has drawn **citizen-petitioned articles** (delay removal / preserve in place). You do **not**
draft these — petitioners do, under **RSA 39:3** — and you must place any properly submitted petition on
the warrant. Present them **neutrally**; the Select Board may vote a recommendation. Note that the
bridge-aid runway (Article B-1) and the petitioners' "study it for a year" request occupy the **same
timeline** — frame them as compatible, not opposed.

## Ballot-order & strategy checklist

- [ ] Establish the **reserves (Group A) first** — easy majority "yes," commits to little.
- [ ] Place **leverage articles (Group B)** with explicit aid splits and **contingency-on-aid** clauses.
- [ ] Confirm **water system legal status** (town vs. precinct) before B-2.
- [ ] Confirm **bridge inspection currency** and program-list status before B-1.
- [ ] Run all language through **town counsel** and **DRA pre-review**.
- [ ] Attach **recommendation tags**; re-vote recommendations on anything **amended** at the
      deliberative session (RSA 40:13).
- [ ] Pair the warrant with the **CIP** and the [deck](./deliberative-session-deck.md) at a
      pre-session information night — you cannot explain at the ballot box.

> Reminder: capital reserve deposits are **appropriations**. In an SB2 town, if the operating budget
> article fails and the default budget takes effect, separately-warranted special articles (these)
> still stand on their own ballot lines — but communicate the linkage so voters see the whole plan.
