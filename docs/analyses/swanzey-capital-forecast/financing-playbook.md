# NH Municipal Capital Financing Playbook

A reference companion to the Capital Forecast Book. Every tool a NH administrator stacks to fund
capital — with the rule that actually governs each, and the move that uses it. Verify current statute
text at [gencourt.state.nh.us](https://gc.nh.gov/rsa/html/nhtoc.htm) and program terms with the agency.

---

## The funding ladder (always top-down)

> Spend the **last** local dollar, not the first. For every project, climb the ladder until the local
> share is as small as the law allows, then fund *that* with reserves.

| Rung | Tool | Why it's higher on the ladder |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | State/federal categorical aid (bridge aid, block grant) | Dedicated money you forfeit if unused |
| 2 | Enterprise / revolving funds (DWSRF/CWSRF) | Repaid by rates, off the property tax |
| 3 | Competitive grants + principal forgiveness (CDBG, NHDES AM) | Free money, but must compete/apply |
| 4 | Capital reserves (RSA 35) | Local, but **majority vote** and zero interest |
| 5 | Bonding (RSA 33 / Bond Bank 35-A) | Last resort: interest cost + **3/5** vote |

---

## Tool 1 — Capital Reserve Funds (RSA 35)

- **Vote:** simple **majority** to establish and to fund — the single most important fact in this book.
- **Custody:** Trustees of Trust Funds (RSA 31:22); invested under RSA 35:9.
- **Purpose:** a specific capital improvement/equipment, or a *type* of improvement/equipment
  (RSA 35:1). Broader purpose = more flexibility.
- **Spending:** the meeting appropriates *from* the fund; or it may name an **agent to expend**
  (often the select board) so a later withdrawal needs no new meeting vote.
- **Move:** stand up one reserve per major asset class today; deposit annually; this is the CIP's bank.
- **Cousin tools:** **expendable trust funds** (RSA 31:19-a) for flexible, repair-type reserves;
  **revolving funds** (RSA 31:95-h) for fee-supported services. Pick the right tool for the cash-flow
  pattern.

## Tool 2 — State Bridge Aid (RSA 234)

- **Split:** **80% state / 20% municipal** of eligible construction/reconstruction, subject to annual
  funding and the program queue.
- **Eligibility:** clear span **≥ 10 feet**; **biennial bridge inspection** compliance is a
  prerequisite to apply.
- **Red list:** NBI primary-element condition **≤ 4**; NHDOT notifies towns of their red-list bridges
  by **April 1** each year — being red-listed raises priority.
- **Process:** municipally-managed projects run through NHDOT's Local Public Agency program.
- **Move:** keep inspections current, apply early, and **bank the 20% share** in a bridge reserve while
  you wait — so you're shovel-ready the year aid lands.

## Tool 3 — Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (NHDES DWSRF)

- **Loan terms:** **1% interest during construction**; repayment begins ~**1 year after substantial
  completion**; long amortization.
- **Principal forgiveness:** for **disadvantaged** systems / where water rates exceed the statewide
  affordability criteria — real grant-equivalent subsidy.
- **Up-front grants:** **asset-management** grants and **strategic-planning** grants fund preliminary
  engineering reports / hydrogeologic studies.
- **Process:** **spring pre-application**; ranked on the **Intended Use Plan** → project priority list;
  funded assets require an **Asset Management & Renewal Plan (AMRP)**.
- **Move:** grant-fund the study, pre-apply, repay through **water rates** (do a rate study), chase
  principal forgiveness — and the project largely **leaves the property tax**.
- **Sibling:** **Clean Water SRF** for wastewater; **PFAS** grant/loan fund if contamination is in play.

## Tool 4 — Bonding (RSA 33) & the NH Municipal Bond Bank (RSA 35-A)

- **Vote:** long-term debt needs **3/5** in SB2 towns (2/3 in traditional town meeting).
- **Bond Bank (RSA 35-A):** pools municipal borrowing for **lower rates** and access; the standard
  channel when a town must borrow.
- **Discipline:** debt-service-to-budget ratios; a published CIP + funded reserves **improve the bond
  rating** and the odds of a 3/5 "yes."
- **Move:** in a healthy plan you don't bond at all. Reserve it for genuine emergencies — and note that
  a reserve-funded town that *can* pay-go negotiates from strength if it ever does borrow.

## Tool 5 — Highway Block Grant (RSA 235) & road money

- Annual state aid for Class IV/V road work, formula-distributed.
- **Move:** don't dribble it across patching — **concentrate** a share into a roads reserve to fund a
  real reconstruction (Lower Winchester Street) without a separate tax ask.

## Tool 6 — CDBG & community grants

- Community Development Block Grants fund accessibility, housing, and public-facility projects (the ADA
  stairwell).
- **Move:** pair a CDBG with a small facilities-reserve match; 50% off a $250k project is a $125k local
  problem.

## Tool 7 — Value-capture & incentive tools

- **TIF districts (RSA 162-K)** — finance infrastructure from incremental value in a defined district.
- **79-E Community Revitalization Tax Relief** — incentivize private rehab in village centers.
- **Betterment assessments** — charge benefiting properties for a localized improvement.
- **Move:** use where growth or a specific benefit district exists (e.g., a commercial corridor) so the
  beneficiaries, not the whole tax base, carry the cost.

## The administrator's capital-finance reflexes

- **"Whose money can pay for this before ours?"** — ask it for every project, every time.
- **Reserves beat bonds** in an SB2 town: majority vs. 3/5, $0 interest vs. interest.
- **Enterprise/SRF beats tax** for water/sewer: rates, not the rate.
- **A published CIP is a financing tool**, not paperwork — it converts surprises into expectations.
- **Bank shares while you queue** — be shovel-ready the year the aid arrives.
- **Smooth the glide** — voters forgive a small steady climb; they revolt at a spike.
