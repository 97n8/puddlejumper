# The New Hampshire Municipal Year — Month by Month

The administrator's metronome. Dates assume a **calendar fiscal year** (Jan 1–Dec 31, the NH default)
and an **SB2** town with a March voting session; adjust for July–June fiscal years and traditional
town meeting. Statutory deadlines are **hard** — DRA cannot set a rate on missing forms. Swanzey
anchors `[CASE]` are noted.

> Legend: **[STAT]** statutory/DRA deadline · **[OPS]** operational milestone · **[BOARD]** board
> action · **[PUBLIC]** public-facing.

---

## January

- **[OPS]** New fiscal year underway; open new-year purchase orders, encumbrances carried forward.
- **[OPS]** Finalize the proposed budget and the **default budget** (RSA 40:13 IX) for SB2.
- **[BOARD]** Board finalizes warrant-article recommendations and recommendation tags.
- **[STAT/PUBLIC]** Hold the **budget public hearing** (RSA 32:5; RSA 40:13 II-a for SB2) — the default
  budget must be disclosed here on the DRA form.
- **[STAT]** **Post the warrant and budget** with the warrant (RSA 31:95), within statutory notice.
- **[OPS]** Issue W-2s/1099s; year-end payroll close.

## February

- **[PUBLIC]** **SB2 First (Deliberative) Session** — debate and amend the budget and articles.
  *Swanzey: first Tuesday in February* `[CASE]`. Articles go to the ballot **as amended**.
- **[BOARD]** Re-vote recommendations on any article amended at the deliberative session.
- **[OPS]** Finalize ballot language with the clerk and printer; absentee ballots prepared.
- **[OPS]** Begin closing the **prior** fiscal year books for the auditor.

## March

- **[STAT/PUBLIC]** **SB2 Second (Voting) Session / Election Day** — official ballot vote on budget,
  articles, and **officer elections**. *Swanzey: second Tuesday in March* `[CASE]`.
- **[OPS]** Tally; prepare for any **recount** requests (Swanzey's 2026 public-works article went to a
  recount, 681–485 `[CASE]`).
- **[BOARD]** Organizational meeting: elect chair, set meeting schedule, appoint/reappoint, designate
  signatories.

## April

- **[STAT]** **File MS-232 "Appropriations as Voted" within 20 days** of the meeting, with certified
  minutes. DRA verifies article language against the minutes and disallows defective articles.
- **[OPS]** Implement what passed: hire, contract, start capital projects, set up new reserve funds
  with the Trustees of Trust Funds.
- **[OPS]** External **audit** fieldwork (often spring) on the prior fiscal year.

## May

- **[OPS]** First-quarter budget-vs-actual review with department heads; early variance flags.
- **[BOARD]** Set board goals for the year; begin the TA's own goal-setting for the annual evaluation.
- **[OPS]** Spring road program; bid public-works projects under the local purchasing policy.

## June

- **[OPS/PUBLIC]** Issue the **first-half tax bill** (semi-annual towns, RSA 76:15-a) — estimated at
  half the prior year's full-year tax.
- **[NHMA]** **MMANH annual conference** (June) — professional development and peer network `[NHMA]`.
- **[OPS]** Mid-year financial review; project year-end fund balance.

## July

- **[OPS]** Begin the **next-year budget build**: issue templates and calendar to department heads.
- **[OPS]** Capital projects in construction season; monitor encumbrances and grant draws.
- **[OPS]** Update the **CIP** (RSA 674:5–8) with the committee for the coming cycle.

## August

- **[OPS]** Department budget requests due back; first reconciliation against revenue forecast and CIP.
- **[OPS]** Prepare **MS-1 (Summary of Valuation)** data with the assessor; finalize exemptions/credits
  (RSA 72) and any revaluation updates.
- **[OPS]** Equalization data to DRA as requested (RSA 21-J).

## September

- **[STAT]** **MS-1 due September 1** (Summary of Valuation), with **MS-434** (revised revenues) and
  **MS-535** (financial report) per DRA schedule.
- **[OPS]** Build the draft budget; identify capital and special warrant articles for the warrant.
- **[BOARD]** First board budget workshops.

## October

- **[OPS]** DRA reviews submissions; respond to any MS-1/valuation questions promptly — the rate can't
  be set until DRA is satisfied.
- **[BOARD]** Budget workshops continue; CIP priorities sequenced into the warrant.
- **[OPS]** Optional DRA **warrant-article pre-review** for language compliance — use it.

## November

- **[STAT/OPS]** **DRA sets the tax rate** (municipal + county + state ed/SWEPT + local ed), applying
  the equalization ratio and overlay (RSA 76:6). Swanzey FY2025: $20.18 total `[CASE]`.
- **[OPS/PUBLIC]** Issue the **second-half (final) tax bill** once the rate is set.
- **[BOARD]** Near-final budget and default-budget figures to the board.

## December

- **[OPS]** Year-end close prep: encumbrances (RSA 32:7), lapse analysis, fund-balance projection.
- **[STAT/PUBLIC]** Finalize the warrant, budget, and **default budget**; schedule the January public
  hearing and the February deliberative session.
- **[BOARD]** Adopt the proposed warrant; the cycle restarts.

## The five deadlines you never miss

| Deadline | Form / action | Statute / source |
|---|---|---|
| **September 1** | MS-1 Summary of Valuation | DRA `[STAT]` |
| **Meeting + 20 days** | MS-232 Appropriations as Voted | DRA `[STAT]` |
| **Before posting warrant** | Budget public hearing + default-budget disclosure | RSA 32:5 / 40:13 II-a |
| **With the warrant** | Post budget | RSA 31:95 |
| **Within 20 days (budget-committee towns)** | MS-737 certified by committee majority | RSA 32:14–24 |

## The four numbers you always know

1. **Total operating budget** and YoY % (Swanzey FY2026: $8.94M, +6.8% `[CASE]`).
2. **Total tax rate** and your **municipal** slice (Swanzey: $20.18; municipal $5.62 `[CASE]`).
3. **Unassigned fund balance** and the **% target** (DRA: ~5–17%).
4. **The next statutory deadline** — always.
