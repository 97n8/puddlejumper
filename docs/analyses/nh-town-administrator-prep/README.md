# The Town Administrator Preparation Workbook (New Hampshire)

**The world's most complete prep system for the NH municipal chief-administrative-officer role.**

Built to take a serious candidate from "I understand local government" to "I can run a New Hampshire
town meeting cycle, set a tax rate, survive a Right-to-Know request, and manage a select board" —
with the **Town of Swanzey** as the running worked case.

---

## How to use this workbook

Work it in three passes:

1. **Read** `WORKBOOK.md` start to finish (Parts I–III, 15 modules). This is the spine.
2. **Drill** with `interview-bank.md` (90 questions, model answers, STAR scaffolds) and
   `flashcards.md` (rapid statutory recall).
3. **Operationalize** with `annual-cycle.md` (the NH municipal year, month-by-month, every DRA
   deadline) and `first-100-days.md` (your onboarding plan).

Keep `statutory-crosswalk.csv` open the whole time — it is the RSA map every NH administrator carries
in their head.

## A note on authority and accuracy

New Hampshire statutes (RSA) are amended regularly. Every citation here is given so you can
**verify the current text** at [gencourt.state.nh.us](https://gc.nh.gov/rsa/html/nhtoc.htm) — a real
administrator never quotes a statute they haven't re-read. NHMA's *Knowing the Territory* handbook and
the DRA *Technical Assistance* brochures are the canonical companions; this workbook organizes and
pressure-tests that knowledge, it does not replace the source texts.

> Built in the PublicLogic house style: every claim traceable, fail-closed on anything you'd have to
> bluff. If you can't cite it, you don't know it yet.
