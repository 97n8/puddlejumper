# Flashcards — Rapid Recall

Cover the answer, say it out loud, check. Cycle daily until automatic. Format: **Q** → *A*.
Verify any statute against the current text before relying on it.

---

## Authority & structure

- **Q: Is "Town Administrator" a statutory office in NH?** → *No. "Town Manager" is (RSA 37). An
  administrator has no independent statutory authority; the select board retains all RSA 41 authority
  and delegates.*
- **Q: What 4 things can a town manager NOT do (RSA 37)?** → *Warn town meetings, make bylaws, borrow
  money, assess/collect taxes.*
- **Q: Who is the governing body of a NH town?** → *The select board (RSA 41:8 — "manage the prudential
  affairs of the town").*
- **Q: Who holds legislative power in a town?** → *The voters, at town meeting / SB2 ballot.*
- **Q: Two officials who are usually ELECTED, not your reports?** → *Town Clerk and Tax Collector
  (also moderator, trustees).*
- **Q: Who do you serve?** → *The board as a body, by majority vote — not any single member.*

## Budget & finance

- **Q: Governing budget statute?** → *RSA 32, Municipal Budget Law.*
- **Q: MS-636 vs MS-737?** → *636 = no budget committee; 737 = official budget committee (certified by
  committee majority, filed within 20 days of meeting).*
- **Q: RSA 32:8?** → *No expenditure in excess of the appropriation for that purpose.*
- **Q: RSA 32:10?** → *Transfers between appropriations within the total (unless voted line-item).*
- **Q: RSA 32:7?** → *Appropriations lapse at year-end unless encumbered or voted non-lapsing.*
- **Q: RSA 32:11?** → *Emergency over-expenditure — requires DRA approval.*
- **Q: Recommended unassigned fund balance range?** → *~5–17% of operating budget (DRA guidance).*
- **Q: Supermajority to bond?** → *3/5 of the meeting (RSA 33).*
- **Q: What does "apply fund balance" do?** → *Lowers this year's tax rate but draws down the cushion;
  manage as multi-year smoothing.*

## Tax rate & the year

- **Q: Four components of the NH property tax rate?** → *Municipal + county + state education (SWEPT) +
  local education.*
- **Q: Who sets the rate?** → *DRA (not the town).*
- **Q: MS-1 due date?** → *September 1.*
- **Q: MS-232 due?** → *Within 20 days after the meeting (Appropriations as Voted).*
- **Q: What is the overlay?** → *Cushion raised for abatements/uncollected taxes (RSA 76:6).*
- **Q: Swanzey FY2025 total rate / municipal slice?** → *$20.18 total; $5.62 municipal.*
- **Q: Swanzey FY2026 operating budget / change?** → *$8.94M, ~+6.8%.*

## SB2 / town meeting

- **Q: Statute for SB2?** → *RSA 40:13.*
- **Q: Two SB2 sessions?** → *Deliberative (debate/amend) and voting (official ballot).*
- **Q: Can a deliberative-session amendment eliminate an article's subject?** → *No — amounts/language
  yes, subject matter no.*
- **Q: What is the default budget?** → *RSA 40:13 IX — prior appropriations adjusted for obligations
  minus one-time items; applies if the operating-budget article fails.*
- **Q: Who calculates the default budget?** → *The select board, unless delegated (3/5) to the budget
  committee.*
- **Q: Where must the default budget be disclosed?** → *At the first budget public hearing (RSA 40:13
  II-a).*
- **Q: Can the default budget exceed the proposed budget?** → *Yes.*

## Right-to-Know & records

- **Q: RSA 91-A response window?** → *5 business days.*
- **Q: Default posture for records?** → *Public unless the body proves an exemption.*
- **Q: Can a board quorum decide town business by email?** → *No — that's an illegal meeting under
  RSA 91-A.*
- **Q: Nonpublic-session statute and a key reason?** → *RSA 91-A:3; e.g., dismissal/compensation of an
  employee, litigation with counsel, certain real estate.*
- **Q: Retention statute?** → *RSA 33-A (33-A:3-a, 156 categories).*
- **Q: Retention for meeting minutes?** → *Permanent.*
- **Q: Can you delete a record early to save space?** → *No — not before the retention period expires.*

## Personnel / labor

- **Q: Collective-bargaining statute?** → *RSA 273-A (PELRB).*
- **Q: Who funds CBA cost items?** → *The legislative body (voters).*
- **Q: Retirement system statute?** → *RSA 100-A (Group I employees; Group II police/fire).*

## Operations / land use / welfare

- **Q: Is there a NH town procurement statute?** → *No single one — you run on a LOCAL purchasing
  policy.*
- **Q: Mandatory local welfare statute?** → *RSA 165 (general assistance; written guidelines required).*
- **Q: Emergency management statute?** → *RSA 21-P.*
- **Q: Road classifications?** → *RSA 231, Class I–VI.*
- **Q: CIP statute?** → *RSA 674:5–8 (6+-year rolling capital plan).*
- **Q: Two economic-development tax tools?** → *TIF (RSA 162-K) and 79-E community revitalization tax
  relief.*

## The reflexes (say these without thinking)

- **If I can't cite it, I don't know it yet — so I call counsel or NHMA.**
