## Cross-cutting flags (apply to every brief)

1. **Non-contingent fees on federal money.** Several briefs carry performance/contingent grant fees (Michigan 5%, AED underwriting). For *federal* awards, restructure to **fixed/non-contingent** (2 CFR §200.459(b)); contingent success fees are acceptable only on *private/non-federal* capital and even then watch broker-dealer/securities lines.
2. **Securities lane.** On capital-raise deals (EM&S, AED), PL provides **governance**, not placement — recommend securities counsel, never solicit investors on commission.
3. **Independence / pay-to-play.** Where a business funds PL to work with a town, the town's interest is the deliverable; disclose who pays; PL never makes the town's decisions; client owns everything (see Business Plan §7).
4. **Founder bandwidth + campaign.** Nate's 2nd-Worcester State Rep run (Election 11/3/26) overlaps Gardner/Phillipston/Fitchburg — manage **c.268A appearance** on home-district municipal deals and protect delivery capacity.
5. **Reconcile-before-you-send.** Multiple deals have conflicting internal numbers (Michigan 3-version proforma; AED job counts). PL's brand *is* reconciliation — fix internal inconsistencies before anything goes to a CDE/investor/regulator.
