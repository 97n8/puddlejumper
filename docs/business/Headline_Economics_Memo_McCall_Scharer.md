## The two problems baked into the $30.2M headline

**1. ~$20.4M/site of it is unsecured tax credits.** RIN (D4/D7) + §45Z make up **$20,428,560** of the signed
revenue. None of it is registered, contracted, or counsel-reviewed. Glen — I still need the line-by-line build-up:
RIN volume + price, §45Z rate, eligibility basis, and whether any of it is registered/contracted or still contingent.

**2. ~$4.8M/site of it is understated operating cost.** The WasteWerx staffing pro forma you sent shows **62 FTE /
$5,522,400 in loaded labor alone** — which is *larger than the entire operating cost the signed model assumed*
($5.38M). Real bottom-up opex is ~$10.2M/site (labor + royalty + monitoring is already a ~$7.0M known floor before
utilities, maintenance, insurance, contracted security/IT/MRV, and G&A — none of which are in the staffing sheet).

**Strip both and the defensible base EBITDA is ~$6.7M/site — not $30.2M.**

## The recommended investor headline

- **Lead with base EBITDA** (fuel + carbon black): ~$6.7M conservative, ~$15.1M if the SAF upgrade and offtake hold.
- **Show RIN + §45Z as clearly-labeled contingent upside** (~$20.4M/site), with a one-line footnote: *eligibility,
  registration, monetization, and counsel/tax review required.*
- **Do not blend** the contingent credits into the headline EBITDA.

This keeps the opportunity big — a 3-site network at base is still ~$20–45M EBITDA, with material credit upside on top —
without making the whole deck depend on credits and a margin that won't survive diligence.
