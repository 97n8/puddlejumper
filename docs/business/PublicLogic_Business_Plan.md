## 0. Reconciliation note (read first)

Two business models exist in PublicLogic's own materials, and this plan reconciles them honestly:

  ($30K–$300K/yr per municipality), FormKey/SEAL/PublicInsight product stack, 19,000-municipality TAM, ~$250M revenue

- **The actual pipeline** (Pocomoke/AED, Michigan LTC, Sutton, Shrewsbury, Phillipston, Fitchburg) is a
  **business-funded and grant-funded governance/compliance *services* model** — PL's non-contingent fee rides inside

**This plan leads with the services model (what's working and paying), and treats the licensing model as Phase 3 —
the scale the services revenue funds and the VAULT module library makes possible.** Don't run the licensing financials
as if they were today's business; run the services pipeline, and let it earn the right to productize.

## 1. What PublicLogic is

**PublicLogic builds and stewards the governance and compliance infrastructure that keeps complex, multi-party,
multi-year projects and institutions aligned across funding sources, permits, commitments, and the 7–12-year
compliance tail — so they survive turnover.** It is sold two ways:

- **B2B2G (business-funded):** a developer/sponsor funds PL to build the governance + grant-stacking + compliance
  spine of a capital-stack deal (NMTC/45Q/OZ/brownfield), as a **capitalized soft cost** in the project budget.
- **Direct municipal (grant- or own-source-funded):** a MA town engages PL (sole-source under **MGL c.30B §7**, or
  via a state grant like Community Compact) for a Map/Diagnostic, a VAULT build, or a CIP.

The engine is **VAULT** (Verification · Authority · Utility · Legitimacy · Transfer) — "not software; the governance
structure underneath the software." Product/tool layers: **LogicOS, CivicPulse, PuddleJumper** (runtime/platform),
**FormKey/SEAL** (intake + submission integrity), **PublicInsight** (Rothschild's behavioral-intelligence layer).
Two principals, one engagement: municipal-policy + code (Boudreau) and behavioral/organizational systems (Rothschild).

## 2. The problem

Multi-site, multi-partner projects and turnover-prone municipalities **fail not from bad vision but from broken
coordination over time**: knowledge lives in individuals, compliance deadlines slip, revenue distributions get
disputed, community commitments erode, leadership transitions break continuity. A 7–25-year obligation cannot run on
relationships and institutional memory. No one sells the coordination layer — clients buy IT, law, engineering, and
consulting separately, and the seams are where projects die.

## 3. The solution & the moat

PL is the single coordination layer on top of all of it: one governed system of record (PuddleJumper/VAULT) that
tracks every permit, deadline, authority chain, and commitment, designed to survive the people who built it. **The
moat is the integrated three-in-one** — municipal-policy expertise + custom code by the same person + doctoral
behavioral science — which (a) no single competitor offers and (b) is exactly what justifies **sole-source** award
(c.30B §7 / 2 CFR §200.320(c)(2)). Town/client **owns everything at close** — no lock-in — which is what makes both a
business-funded municipal engagement and a sole-source award defensible. *The moat and the procurement justification
are the same fact.*

## 5. Go-to-market — the two-sided flywheel

> A **business** (or a state grant) funds a cheap **Map/Diagnostic/Phase 1** → it produces the diagnosis **and** the
> grant/capital case → PL builds the governance spine + stacks the capital (business-funded soft cost) → the deal
> closes / the town gets capacity → PL keeps the **multi-year compliance retainer** → each completed engagement is a
> **business reference + a town reference + reusable VAULT modules** → the next deal costs less to serve.

Real entry products and prices already in market: **Phase 1 governance package $18–25K** (Flint); **VAULT Diagnostic
$18–28K** (Sutton); **Stewardship Map ~$35K**; **MA implementation $123K** (Shrewsbury). Retainers: **$6.5–15K/mo**
(deal coordination) and **$10K/mo per active site** (Michigan).

## 7. The load-bearing risks (design around these or they sink it)

1. **Independence / pay-to-play.** Business-funded municipal work is capture if structured carelessly. Rule: the
   *town's* interest is the deliverable; disclose who pays; PL never makes the town's decisions; client owns
   everything. (See `../federal-funding/sole_source_federal_overlay.md`.)
2. **Securities.** EM&S/AED investor structures are likely securities (Howey). PL is **governance, not placement** —
   recommend securities counsel; never solicit investors on commission.
3. **Contingent fees on federal money.** The 5% grant-stacking and grant-underwriting fees are **performance-based** —
   fine on *private* awards, **not allowable** charged to federal awards (2 CFR §200.459(b)). Restructure to
   fixed/non-contingent for any federal grant; keep grant-*writing* on own-source (§200.460).
4. **Founder concentration + the campaign.** Delivery is founder-bound (the moat *and* the bottleneck). Nate is also
   running for **State Representative, 2nd Worcester District (Election 11/3/26)** — the **same towns** (Gardner,
   Phillipston, Fitchburg) as the municipal pipeline. Real **c.268A appearance** exposure and a real time split.
   Mitigation: extra disclosure on home-district deals (or defer Gardner until post-election); protect delivery
   capacity; lean on the VAULT module library and a first hire.
5. **Sponsor dependency / stalls.** AED stalled on an unresponsive sponsor. Treat every deal as a **template**, not a
   dependency; keep multiple channels (Glen, WasteWerx, MA municipal) live so no single stall stops revenue.
6. **Internal number hygiene.** Several deals carry conflicting internal figures (Michigan 3-version proforma; AED job
   counts). PL's brand *is* reconciliation — fix internals before anything reaches a CDE/investor/regulator.

## 9. The path (phased)

**Phase 1 — Proof & cash (now → ~12 mo).** Land and deliver the live pipeline: **Michigan LTC** (EGLE feedback ~6/1 →
July 7 full apps), **Sutton** Diagnostic + Compact app, **Shrewsbury** execution, **Fitchburg** (await rank),
**Phillipston/Millbury**, the **WasteWerx MSA**, and 1–2 of **Glen's** deals. Standardize the **sole-source packet**
(c.30B §7 + federal overlay), the **non-contingent fee** structure, and the **independence/disclosure** template so
every deal is clean by default. *Reference assets: Sutton (TIF), Shrewsbury (fiber), Michigan (capital stack).*

**Phase 2 — Productize & stack retainers (12–30 mo).** Turn each engagement into a **retainer + compliance tail**;
build the **VAULT module library** (PRR, OML, DPW, MS4, CLERK, NMTC-compliance, CIP) so each new client consumes
configuration. Convert connectors (Glen) and OEMs (WasteWerx) into **repeatable channels**. Metric that matters:
**% of revenue recurring.**

**Phase 3 — License & scale (30 mo+).** With a proven module library and a recurring base, offer **VAULT as a licensed
standard** ($30–300K/yr) to the ~19,000-municipality market — the LONG FORM BP's vision, now funded by services
revenue and de-risked by real references, rather than by a speculative raise. Capital (if raised) accelerates; it is
not a prerequisite.

## 10. The model on one line

**Get businesses (and grants) to fund the governance + compliance + grant-stacking spine of capital-stack deals and
town modernizations, ride a fixed non-contingent fee inside money the client already controls, convert every build
into a multi-year compliance retainer, reuse the VAULT module library so the next deal costs less — and let that
recurring services base fund the eventual VAULT licensing standard.** The retainers and the tail are the business;
the entry exists to create them; the license is the endgame they earn.
