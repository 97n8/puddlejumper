# EGLE MI Healthy Climate Challenge #4 ("Go Big, Go Clean") — Fit Assessment & Pivot-to-Win

For the Michigan LTC Network team (Robert McCall / EM&S, Garrett Schopp / National Salvage, Glen Scharer / Carbon
Alliance, Dr. Allison Weiss Rothschild & Nathan Boudreau / PublicLogic). Prepared 2026-06-03. Full application **due
11:59 PM EST, July 7, 2026** to NeuenfeldtH@Michigan.gov. **This is a decision document — read the verdict first.**

---

## 0. Verdict (bottom line up front)

**The LTC Network concept as submitted in the three LOIs is a weak-to-ineligible fit for Challenge #4, and should not
be carried into the full application unchanged.** The program's own LOI feedback and rubric make this unambiguous:

- **It funds a feasibility *study* — "planning activities only," no equipment/construction.** (LTC is a build.)
- **Carbon credits / offset markets / 45Q monetization are *explicitly ineligible*.** (Core LTC value prop.)
- **It rewards ≥50% decarbonization of an *existing* facility's *process*, via process change/electrification** — and
  names biogas/RNG, CHP, CCS, hydrogen as "not a focus on their own." (Pyrolysis waste-to-fuel reads as off-target.)
- **<$400K requested is encouraged; your LOIs asked $420K/site.**

**Recommendation:** **Pivot, don't push.** Re-aim the full application at a feasibility study to deeply decarbonize an
**existing Michigan industrial facility your team already controls** (National Salvage's McBain/Lincoln biomass plants
are the strongest candidate). Keep the partners; change the subject from "our new plant" to "their existing facility's
process emissions." If you can't line up a genuine facility partner + a technical engineering lead by ~June 20, **do
not spend the month writing this one** — put the energy into the NMTC/private-capital track where the LTC build
actually belongs.

*This grant is the wrong instrument for building the plant. It can, however, fund a real study that strengthens the
very facilities in your network — and that study is winnable.*

## 1. What Challenge #4 actually funds (from the uploaded docs)

- **Instrument:** A planning/feasibility **study** (DOE-federal pass-through via EGLE). **"This funding cannot support
  the direct purchase, installation, or construction of equipment or facilities. Funds must support planning
  activities only."**
- **Subject:** An **active, named Michigan industrial facility** whose **own process/energy emissions** the study
  evaluates for **deep decarbonization (≥50%)**, emphasizing **process change and electrification** over CCUS/biogas/
  RNG/CHP/hydrogen.
- **Explicitly ineligible:** **carbon credits, offset markets, carbon accounting** research.
- **Money:** **<$400,000 encouraged**, scalable scope, broken out by task; state may award less. Match not required but
  helps. **Project period Sept 1, 2026 – Aug 31, 2027**; all funds expended by 8/31/27. A-133 single audit if cumulative
  federal funding >$750K.
- **Required package:** Application, Timeline (template), Budget (template), Organizational Capacity Statement,
  Facility letter of commitment (required since the lead applicant ≠ the facility), optional resumes.
- **FOIA warning:** do **not** put proprietary process details/trade secrets in the application.

### Scoring rubric (what actually wins)

| § | Criterion | Weight | What "Strong" requires |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Emissions Impact & Depth of Decarbonization** | **30%** | Quantified GHG analysis vs. a **defined facility baseline (EPA FLIGHT/GHGRP or activity-data)**; ≥1 pathway to **≥50%**; named tools (**AVERT**, EPA combustion factors); near- vs long-term; scenario-based |
| 2 | **Facility Commitment & Likelihood of Use** | **20%** | **Named, active MI facility** + **decision-maker** who will act; confirmed data/staff access; leverage helps |

| 6 | **Team Competence & Project Approach** | 10% | Engineering + energy/emissions + financial-modeling expertise; clear roles; realistic timeline/budget |

## 3. The pivot that fits and can win

**New framing:** *"A feasibility study to identify deep-decarbonization (≥50%) pathways for [National Salvage's McBain
(20 MW) and Lincoln (~18 MW) biomass energy facilities serving Consumers Energy], evaluating process electrification,
efficiency, onsite renewables, and the enabling grid infrastructure — with a community/workforce co-benefit analysis
for the host communities — producing transferable insights for Michigan's biomass/industrial-combustion fleet."*

Why it works:
- **Existing, named MI industrial facilities** with a real operator/decision-maker (Garrett Schopp / National
  Salvage) → scores §2.
- **Two facilities** → multi-facility replicability → scores §5 and the "additional considerations."
- **Process decarbonization of combustion** (electrification of process heat, efficiency, onsite solar + storage,
  grid interconnection, rate/policy scenarios) → directly hits §1 and §4 the way the rubric asks.
- **Community/workforce** (ACS Cooperative, The Warehouse) re-anchored to the *facility's* host community as a **≤5%
  subaward** → scores §3 and is genuinely PL/Allie's strength.
- **Study-only, <$400K, Sept 2026–Aug 2027** → eligible on its face.
- **Strip all carbon-credit/45Q language** from *this* application (it stays in the separate NMTC/private-capital
  track — see §6).

Alternative facility if National Salvage won't commit data/decision-maker: **Geocycle's Coleman tire-processing/TDF
operation** (existing industrial process; study electrification/efficiency vs. its current combustion-coupled TDF
pathway). Either way, **you need one real existing facility partner that signs a commitment letter.**

## 4. Rubric-mapped outline (what to write to score "Strong")

- **§1 Emissions (30%) — win this first.** Pull the facility baseline from **EPA FLIGHT/GHGRP** (biomass plants of this
  size likely report) or build it from fuel/energy records + EPA stationary-combustion factors. Name your tools
  (**AVERT** for any electricity-affecting measure; EPA combustion factors for fuel-switching/efficiency). Present
  **≥1 pathway to ≥50%** (e.g., process electrification + onsite solar + efficiency), distinguish near-term vs
  long-term, and run **scenarios** (gas vs electricity price trajectories, tax-credit continuity, interconnection
  upgrades). This is where an **engineering/energy-modeling partner is non-negotiable.**
- **§2 Facility Commitment (20%).** Named facility + **named decision-maker** (e.g., Garrett Schopp) + a **commitment
  letter** confirming data/staff access and intent to act on findings (e.g., at a capital-planning or equipment-
  replacement decision point). Note any leveraged funds.
- **§3 Community (15%).** Workforce/air-quality/public-health analysis for the host community; **community partner
  subaward ≤5%** (ACS Cooperative / The Warehouse) doing engagement/community-action-plan work **tied to the facility**.
- **§4 Tech & Innovation (15%).** Lead with process electrification + circularity + enabling infrastructure; include
  ≥1 forward-looking/conditional pathway; articulate barriers (cost, interconnection, policy) and what makes them
  viable. (Do **not** center CCS/RNG.)
- **§5 Replicability (10%).** Two facilities + a transferable methodology for MI biomass/combustion facilities; commit
  to ≥1 EGLE knowledge-sharing activity (name a venue, e.g., Michigan Manufacturers Association).
- **§6 Team (10%).** Roles: **technical engineering/energy lead (NEW HIRE/PARTNER)**; PublicLogic = project
  governance/coordination, compliance, deliverable management (VAULT) — this is a legitimate, scoreable role;
  **Dr. Rothschild** = community/workforce analysis + implementation/adoption (CFIR); facility partner = data/decisions.
  Realistic <$400K budget, broken out by task with core vs optional scope.

## 6. How this relates to the rest of the plan (don't lose the build)

This is a **scope correction for one grant, not a retreat from the LTC Network.** The plant build, the NMTC capital
stack, 45Q, and carbon credits all remain live — **in the private-capital / CDE track** (`Engagement_Briefs.md` #1,
`Top10_Opportunities.xlsx`). Challenge #4 is a **study** that, if pivoted, *strengthens* that track: a state-funded,
DOE-backed feasibility study of your partner facilities is exactly the kind of investment-readiness/credibility asset
that de-risks the "but-for" narrative for CEI/USBCDC later. Two different instruments, two different scopes — keep
them separate and each stays clean.

**The go/no-go:** if a real facility partner + engineering lead are in hand by ~June 20 → pivot and write to win. If
not → skip this round, tell Haley you're realigning scope, and put the month into the NMTC track and the next EGLE
cycle.
