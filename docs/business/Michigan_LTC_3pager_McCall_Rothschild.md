## Page 1 — The engagement and the governance spine

**The platform.** The Michigan LTC Network is a multi-site circular-economy platform converting end-of-life tires,
railroad ties, and forestry biomass into pyrolysis oil, carbon black, and carbon credits across the Coleman–Flint
corridor. Three to four sites; per-site development cost **$12.2–15M**, per-site revenue **~$16.875M**; network
investment **$45–60M**. Sites: **Coldwater Road** (Genesee Twp, 118 ac, RACER #13270, NFA-complete — substantially
pre-cleared), **Dort Highway** (Flint, 14 ac, brownfield), **Coleman** (co-located with Geocycle). The capital stack
layers EGLE Scrap Tire, EPA Brownfield, **NMTC** (permanent under OBBBA), **45Q** ($85/ton), **Opportunity Zones**
(permanent), USDA REAP/B&I, Brownfield TIF (Act 381), NextCycle MI, and EDA Public Works.

**What PublicLogic is in this network.** Not a vendor on the project — the **governance, compliance, and
implementation infrastructure** that holds a multi-site, multi-partner, 25-year platform together over time.
Industrial platforms like this fail not because of technology but because of **institutional fragility**: knowledge
lives in individuals, compliance deadlines slip, revenue distributions get disputed, community commitments erode, and
leadership transitions break continuity. PublicLogic builds the VAULT framework (Verification · Authority · Utility ·
Legitimacy · Transfer) that tracks every permit, deadline, authority chain, and commitment — designed to survive the
people who built it.

**Where we are (status, 2026-06-03).**
- **Phase-1 VAULT assessment delivered (5/15/26).** It identified **14 action items (5 critical)** — three conflicting
  financial models (~$8M revenue gap), 45.5% of Dort Highway revenue unallocated, an investor-return structure that
  likely constitutes a security, no documented EM&S authority/operating agreement, and an unconfirmed LARA
  registration. *These are not problems we created — they are the structural gaps we surface before regulators,
  investors, or partners see them. That is the value.*
- **Three Letters of Intent submitted to the MI Healthy Climate Challenge #4 ("Go Big, Go Clean") via the EGLE portal
  on 5/19/26** — Lincoln, Flint, and Coleman — **$420K/site × 3 = $1.26M** against the $1.8M pool. **"Encouraged to
  Apply" feedback is expected ~June 1; full applications, by invitation, are due July 7.**
- External validation: **Brien Walton (Acadia Capital)** independently named the governance/ownership structure — i.e.,
  PublicLogic's role — the critical success factor (5/8/26).

**The division of labor this brief aligns:** Robert drives the development, the deal, and investor relations. Allie
owns the human-systems and community-durability layer that makes the social license, the permitting, and the 25-year

## Page 2 — Allie's role: the human-systems and community spine

On a single-building grant this would be a footnote. On a **25-year, multi-site, multi-partner platform with a 10%
community-equity commitment**, the human-systems and community-durability work is load-bearing — and it is exactly
Dr. Rothschild's discipline (PsyD; LICSW, BCBA, LABA; doctoral research on reducing staff turnover in institutional
settings; CFIR implementation science).

**1. The TRANSFER gap is the platform's biggest single risk — and it's Allie's wheelhouse.** The Phase-1 assessment
flagged *"all knowledge in McCall"* as a **critical succession risk**: a 25-year platform must survive 2–3 leadership
transitions. Robert cannot be the single point of institutional memory. Allie designs the **institutional-continuity
protocol** — load-bearing-role identification, internal system stewards, and documented knowledge transfer — so the
network keeps running through staff and leadership change. This is the "T" in VAULT made real.

**2. CFIR-structured implementation across the network.** Allie's method (Consolidated Framework for Implementation
Research — used by NIH/CDC/AHRQ) runs in three phases, deployable per site and reusable across the platform:
- **Outer & Inner Setting assessment** — document how each site/partner actually operates before anything is built;
  surface informal roles, relational connections, and tension-for-change.
- **Implementation-process support** — manage the human side of adoption during deployment (where most projects fail),
  with structured status diagnostics, not progress updates.
- **Sustainability & institutional transfer** — steward designations, independence verification, and the continuity
  documentation that makes the system outlast the engagement.

**3. The community-impact model — make the 10% real, not verbal.** The platform commits **10% of equity** to community
engagement through **ACS Cooperative (Frank Marco)** — hydroponic/aquaponic workforce training and a commercial-kitchen
incubator, with University of Arizona (Dr. Cuello) — and **The Warehouse / Gen 215** (family services, local food
production, Flint). The Phase-1 assessment flagged that this commitment is **currently verbal** — a durability risk.
Allie's role: **encode the community model in the operating agreements** with documented distribution mechanisms and
workforce-outcome tracking, so the social license that enables permitting and the next site is **a governed obligation,
signed and logged**, not a promise. When the City of Flint asked *"how will the project teach people to cook and
prepare the food,"* the ACS commercial-kitchen incubator was the answer — that is the community-systems layer Allie
operationalizes.

**Why this matters to Robert specifically:** the community model and the continuity protocol are not "soft" add-ons —
they are what make the **"but-for" narrative** credible to CDEs, the permitting defensible to EGLE, and the
distributions durable to investors. Allie's work is what lets you tell USBCDC/CEI the benefits aren't projections,
they're governed obligations from day one.

## Page 3 — Money, path, risks, and next steps

**Fees and cost treatment (from the LTC workbook).** PublicLogic's governance/continuity buildout is a **capitalized
development cost inside the project budget — zero net change to project cost**, not an added line item:
- **Site-1 buildout: $175,000** (from contingency). Sites 2–3: **$75K–100K**. Sites 4–10+: **$50K–75K** (template
  deploy — each site cheaper than the last as the module library matures).
- **Runtime: $10,000/month per active site** — replaces reactive legal/compliance spend (~$380K/yr savings).
- **Grant stacking: 5% of awards** (performance-based) — **see flag below**.
- Allie's CFIR continuity and community-systems work is delivered **inside** the buildout + runtime, not billed
  separately — one integrated PublicLogic engagement, unified accountability.

**The path.**
1. **Now → ~June 1:** chase the EGLE "Encouraged to Apply" feedback on the three LOIs.
2. **June → July 7:** if invited, PublicLogic leads the **full applications**; in parallel, close the critical Phase-1
   fixes (single authoritative proforma; EM&S operating agreement; LARA lookup) — these are billable governance work
   *and* gating items for every filing.
3. **Site-1 close → replication:** lock Coldwater/Dort/Coleman site 1, then template the buildout for sites 2–4. Allie
   stands up the continuity protocol and encodes the community model into the operating agreements at close.

**Honest flags (so nothing surprises us later).**
- **Securities lane.** The investor-return structure likely constitutes a security (Howey). PublicLogic stays in the
  **governance** lane — we recommend securities counsel before any investor solicitation and **do not act as a
  placement agent**. (Brien Walton will expect this addressed.)
- **Keep grant fees non-contingent on federal money.** A 5%-of-awards fee is fine on **private** awards, but for
  **federal** grants it must be restructured to **fixed/non-contingent** (2 CFR §200.459(b) disfavors contingent fees
  charged to federal awards). Easy fix; do it before the July 7 federal-touching applications.
- **Reconcile before anything circulates.** Until the three-version proforma reconciles to one authoritative model, no
  investor/CDE/EGLE document should go out. PublicLogic authors the single source of truth.

   continuity, and community-durability; Nate: the governance/compliance spine that connects them.
