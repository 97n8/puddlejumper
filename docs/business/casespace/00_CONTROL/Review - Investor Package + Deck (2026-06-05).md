## Issues, ranked

### 1. CRITICAL — the EBITDA headline is the *unreconciled* signed number. Show the range.
Slide 12 leads with **EBITDA $30,200,531** (and slide 13 with **$90,601,593** network), captioned only "carbon black
excluded / staffing not yet integrated." But you already know — and the package text admits — that the $30.2M sits on
the Pro Forma's **4 FTE + manager ($425,000)**, while the attached WasteWerx staffing pro forma says **62 FTE
($5,522,400)**. Integrate that and the per-site EBITDA is **~$22.0M**, the network **~$66.1M**, and **base fuel
without credits is ~$1.6M**. 

**The fix:** don't present $30.2M with a footnote — present the **reconciled range**:

| Per site | As signed (5-FTE Pro Forma) | Operator-reality (62-FTE) |
|---|---:|---:|
| Steady-state EBITDA | $30,200,531 | **~$22,047,043** |
| Base fuel only (no credits) | $9,771,971 | **~$1,618,483** |

A sophisticated investor *will* ask "who runs this for $425K?" — answer it on the slide, on your terms, before they
ask. Showing the range is more credible than the single number, and it's the posture the rest of your package already
takes. **This is the only change that's non-negotiable before send.** (Our `MichiganLTC_Network_Financial_Model_FINAL.xlsx`
already computes this exactly — drop those two cells into slides 12–13.)

### 2. HIGH — the investor site list contradicts the submitted regulatory record.
The deck **leads with Coldwater** (full slide 6, "primary hub") and makes **Lincoln "optional"** (slide 9). But your
**submitted LOIs (5/19) were Lincoln, Flint, Coleman** — Coldwater is **not** in the regulatory record (it's Phase 2).
Leading the investor deck with a site that isn't in your filed package is a diligence inconsistency. **Fix:** align the
active investor set to **Lincoln / Flint / Coleman** (matching the LOIs), and present Coldwater explicitly as a
*Phase 2 capital opportunity*, not the hero. The `[CONFIRM]` flags the conflict — but the slide order still resolves it
the wrong way.

### 3. HIGH — reconcile the credit-contingency number ($13.4M vs $20.4M).
The package says "**more than $13.4M/yr** depends on RFS/§45Z." But the **canonical Revenue tab line items** (D4 $4,798,080
+ D7 $9,204,480 + §45Z-RD $2,399,040 + §45Z-SAF $4,026,960) sum to **$20,428,560**. Use the canonical **$20.4M** — it's
the larger, more conservative contingency, and it's the sourced number. Stating $13.4M understates how much of the
$35.6M is registration-dependent.

### 4. MEDIUM — the Letters of Support set is mostly DRAFT; don't let the deck imply executed.
The index shows `LOS_AcadiaCapital_DRAFT`, `LOS_LakeStateRailroad_DRAFT`, `LOS_NationalSalvage_DRAFT`,
`LOS_WasteWerx_DRAFT`. Slide 16 lists these as "core partners." Two precision points: **Acadia is advisory validation,
not committed financing**; **Lake State Railway is logistics, not a fuel buyer** (slide 11 gets this right — keep that
discipline on slide 16). Mark draft LOS as draft; don't present partner *support* as partner *commitment*.

### 5. MEDIUM — don't conflate §45Q with §45Z/RIN.
Slide 14 and the package both note a source conflict: a "$4.2M §45Q" planning figure vs the §45Z/RIN operating model.
**45Q is carbon capture; 45Z is clean-fuel** — different mechanisms. The operating revenue uses **45Z + RIN**, not 45Q.
Make sure no slide implies $4.2M of 45Q is banked on top.

### 6. MEDIUM — community benefit: "10% of EBITDA" vs "10% of equity" are wildly different.
The canonical says **10% of EBITDA** (~$3M/yr); the EM&S Investor Brief said **10% of equity**. The deck correctly
**defers the number** (slide 15) — keep it deferred until counsel + sponsor reconcile, because the two readings differ
by an order of magnitude.

### 7. STANDING — securities counsel before any send is not optional.
You're soliciting **$47.5M**. The investor-return structure is itself unreconciled ($3.5M/4yr = $14M vs $5M/5yr+balloon
= $40M — a $26M gap, your reconciliation's Discrepancy #5/6). The deck wisely omits return terms — keep them out until
counsel structures the offering (Reg D + PPM + subs). Slide 18 lists "securities-counsel review"; elevate it from a
checklist line to a **gate**: no investor sees this until that's done.
